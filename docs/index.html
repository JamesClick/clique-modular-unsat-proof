Modular Proof of UNSAT Formulas

Abstract Methodology Theoretical Proof Results Graphs Conclusion

Abstract

We present a novel modular approach to detect the unsatisfiability of logical formulas, using weighted sums over modular arithmetic. This hybrid framework combines polynomial and hash-based weightings with GPU acceleration and big data scalability. The method was tested on unsatisfiable PHP(m,n) formulas and random 3-SAT instances.

Methodology

The method transforms a CNF formula φ into a modular sum:
S(φ) = ∑ rj × residue(Cj) mod M
where each clause Cj is mapped to a residue via assigned primes, and weighted by polynomial or hash-based coefficients rj. We use multiple large moduli (e.g. 10⁶+7, 10⁶+19) to validate the result across independent systems.

Theoretical Proof

We observed that for unsatisfiable formulas, S(φ) ≡ 0 mod M across multiple independent primes and weight functions. In contrast, satisfiable formulas yield non-zero modular residues. The behavior is robust across clause reorderings and structural symmetries. We formalize this under two core lemmas:

  • Lemma 1: UNSAT formulas generate linearly dependent residue vectors.
  • Lemma 2: Chosen weights preserve these dependencies modulo M.
